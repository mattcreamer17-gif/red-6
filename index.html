01THE HYPOTHESIS

The rendering pipeline is not what slows you down. The repeat is.

The F-16 integration is your first operational fighter. Lockheed Mentor-Protege in place since January 2024. Boeing on T-7 and F-15EX. STRATFI burning down ATARS and ARCADE through 2026. Three primes, five airframes on the roadmap, and an engineering team that already solved most of this once on T-38 and MC-130.

The question is how much of that learning is structured, interpretable, and portable across programs, and how much lives in one engineer's head. Eduba builds the layer in between. We call it computational orchestration. Most of what looks like an AI problem is actually three problems stacked. Roughly 60% is traditional code and data, 30% is rule-based logic, 10% is where a model genuinely earns its keep. Teams who skip that split end up with language models writing config files that Postgres would have handled in a millisecond. On an airframe integration program, getting the split right is the difference between a 12-month F-22 integration and an 18-month one.

60%
Layer 01
Traditional code and database work. Bus specs, envelope data, safety-of-flight artifacts.
30%
Layer 02
Rule-based logic. Deconfliction rules, IP boundary policy, certification triggers.
10%
Layer 03
Model-driven. Adversary behavior, tactics generation, dynamic threat scaling.

The methodology is published. The Interpretable Context Methodology, submitted to ACM TiiS, maps context as a layered filesystem (L0 identity through L4 working artifacts) with measurable gains in reproducibility and handoff speed. It is the same shape you would want across T-38, MC-130, F-16, TF-50, F-22, F-35, T-7, and F-15EX: one structure, one vocabulary, learned once and carried forward.

For regulated-industry exposure, Eduba also publishes the Ethics Engine, a psychometric assessment tool for evaluating ideological and moral patterns in LLMs (repo here). Relevant when the "AI-enabled" line in ARCADE starts getting harder questions from USAF program offices and allied program staff.
